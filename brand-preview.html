09 — Pitfalls

Wrong vs right

The four most common Nevron brand mistakes, side by side.

1. Rounded cards soften the brand

Wrong

Soft and generic

16px rounded corners — feels like every SaaS template.

Rounded cards dilute the architectural, precise feel Nevron is built on.

Right

Sharp and confident

0 radius — architectural, on-brand, unmistakably Nevron.

Use --nevron-radius-lg (value 0) for every card. Always.

2. Never recreate the logo in code

Wrong
NEVRON

Re-typing "NEVRON" in a condensed font or re-drawing the SVG paths from memory produces garbled letterforms — the custom ligatures and proportions are lost.

Right

Copy the source SVG from assets/logos/ into your project and reference it via <img>.

3. Pure black text fatigues on long reads

Wrong

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Pure #000000 on white.

#000000 on #FFFFFF is harsh on content-heavy pages and feels flat.

Right

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. S1 #080C13 on white.

S1 is warmer, more Nevron, and still passes WCAG AAA for body text.

4. Gradients stay within the blue family

Wrong
Red + blue gradient

Supporting colors (red, orange, yellow, green) are functional only — never decorative. Mixing with blues breaks the brand.

Right
M3 · M4 · M5 blue gradient

Use --nevron-gradient-blue for accents. For dark contexts, use --nevron-gradient-dark.
